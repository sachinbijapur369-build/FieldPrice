What Lubricants Do

The six functions of industrial lubrication.

The right lubricant does more than reduce friction. Using the wrong grade or type — even from a premium brand — can cost significantly more than any price saving.

Reduces Friction & Wear

Creates a separating film between metal surfaces, extending component life by 3–10× compared to inadequate lubrication.

Controls Temperature

Carries heat away from bearings and hydraulic components. In high-speed systems, thermal management is as critical as friction reduction.

Prevents Corrosion

Forms a protective barrier against moisture and atmospheric corrosion. Essential for equipment in humid or chemical environments.

Transmits Power

In hydraulic systems, the oil is the working fluid. The wrong viscosity directly reduces machine power output and response speed.

Keeps Systems Clean

Dispersant additives carry contaminants to filters. Incorrect oil leaves sludge deposits that block passages and accelerate wear.

Reduces Downtime

Correctly lubricated equipment runs longer between service intervals. Unplanned breakdowns cost 3–5× more than planned maintenance.

Buyer's Guide

How to source the right lubricant at a fair price.

1

Identify the application first.

The application determines the lubricant type — hydraulic system, gearbox, compressor, bearing housing or engine all require fundamentally different products. Substituting types because something is available is one of the most costly maintenance errors in industry.

💡 Tip: Share the machine manual page — we will identify the correct type before quoting.
2

Confirm the viscosity grade from the OEM manual.

Viscosity grade — ISO VG 32, 46, 68 for industrial; SAE 10W-40 for engines — is always specified by the original equipment manufacturer. Never substitute grades without engineering confirmation. Consequences range from accelerated wear to complete machine damage.

💡 Tip: ISO grades for industrial machines. SAE grades for automotive and engine applications.
3

Check for OEM approval requirements.

Imported equipment often requires lubricants meeting specific OEM approvals beyond viscosity — "Meets Bosch Rexroth HLP" or "Approved for Atlas Copco compressors." These must be matched precisely to preserve equipment warranties.

💡 Tip: Share the OEM approval spec — we verify compliance before quoting any product.
4

Calculate consumption and order in bulk.

Full drums (208L) are consistently cheaper per litre than cartons or pails. If storage allows, buying in bulk is the single largest lever for reducing per-unit cost — independent of which distributor you use.

Product Types

Types of bearings and when to use each.

An incorrectly specified bearing will fail under load regardless of brand or price. Specification must precede brand selection.

Deep Groove Ball Bearings

Most common type. Radial and moderate axial loads. Motors, fans and gearboxes. SKF 6000/6200/6300 series are universal standards.

Tapered Roller Bearings

Combined radial and heavy axial loads. Essential for shafts and axles. Timken is the global authority. Always installed in opposing pairs.

Cylindrical Roller Bearings

High radial capacity, low friction. Paper mills, rolling mills and heavy gearboxes. Do not use where significant thrust loads are present.

Spherical Roller Bearings

Self-aligning. Ideal where shaft deflection is expected — conveyor pulleys, fans and heavy vibrating equipment.

Thrust Ball Bearings

Exclusively for axial loads — vertical shafts, screw jacks, cranes. Cannot carry radial loads under any circumstances.

Needle Roller Bearings

Very high radial capacity in compact space. Gearbox needle cages, rocker pivots and cam followers. INA is the category specialist.

Buyer's Guide

How to source the right bearing at the right price.

1

Find the full designation stamped on the bearing.

Every bearing has a designation stamped on its outer ring — "6205 2RS" or "NU 310 ECM". This fully defines the bearing across all brands. Photograph it before discarding the old bearing. Most sourcing errors begin with an incorrect or incomplete part number.

💡 Tip: If you cannot find the designation, share the machine make and model — we will identify it.
2

Understand the suffix — it changes the bearing.

"2RS" means rubber sealed. "ZZ" means metal shielded. "C3" means extra internal clearance — required for high-temperature or press-fit applications. A 6205 2RS and a 6205 2RS C3 are different bearings and are not interchangeable. The wrong suffix causes premature failure.

💡 Tip: Always share the complete designation including all suffix codes. We verify before quoting.
3

Insist on authorised supply with traceable provenance.

Bearing counterfeiting is extensive in India. Fake SKF and NSK bearings are visually indistinguishable from genuine products and fail under operating load — often catastrophically. There is no compromise on authorised sourcing for critical machinery. We source exclusively through authorised channels.

💡 Tip: If a supplier cannot confirm the authorised distributor chain, do not proceed with that purchase.
4

Validate the price before ordering.

💡 Tip: Part number and quantity is all we need. Verified market comparison by end of business day.

Product Types

Types of drive belts and when to use each.

Using the wrong belt type causes premature failure regardless of brand quality. Specification always comes before brand selection.

Classical V-Belts (A/B/C/D)

Most common type. Used in fans, pumps and compressors. Cross-section must exactly match the pulley groove.

Narrow V-Belts (SPZ/SPA/SPB/SPC)

Higher power density. Run cooler, transmit more power per unit width. Preferred for high-efficiency drives and retrofits.

Synchronous / Timing Belts

Toothed belts for precise timing. Zero slip. Tooth profile must match exactly — HTD, GT and AT are not interchangeable.

Poly-V / Ribbed Belts

Multiple V-ribs on a flat back. High flexibility, smooth operation. Common in HVAC and agricultural equipment.

Flat Belts

For high-speed, long centre-distance drives. Textile, wood processing and paper mills. Precise tensioning is critical.

Banded / Joined Belts

Multiple V-belts joined at the top. Used where pulsating loads cause individual belts to jump — mining, heavy crushers.

Buyer's Guide

How to source the right belt without overpaying.

1

Find the designation printed on the old belt.

Every belt has a designation printed on it — "B-82", "SPB-2240", "8MGT-2240-30". Photograph it before discarding. This reference is consistent across all brands and prevents most sourcing errors.

💡 Tip: No old belt? Share the machine make and model — we will identify the correct designation.
2

Verify cross-brand compatibility before substituting.

Standard designations (A, B, SPA, SPB, SPZ) are interchangeable across major brands. However some European OEM equipment uses proprietary profiles — Optibelt and Habasit in particular. We maintain cross-reference charts for all major brands.

💡 Tip: Do not assume a standard designation applies to OEM equipment without verification.
3

Replace all belts in a multi-belt drive simultaneously.

Mixing old and new belts causes uneven load distribution — the new belt carries disproportionate load and fails early. One of the most common and preventable maintenance errors we encounter.

💡 Tip: Ask us for matched set pricing — replacing all belts together is typically more economical.
4

Validate the price before ordering.

Import brand belts — particularly Gates and Continental — carry high distributor margins in India. The same designation can vary 30% or more. Send us the part number and quantity before placing any order.

💡 Tip: Part number and quantity is all we need. Verified price by end of business day.

Product Types

Types of air compressors and when to use each.

The wrong compressor technology significantly increases total cost of ownership. Selection must be based on air demand, duty cycle and quality requirements.

Rotary Screw Compressors

Most efficient for continuous duty industrial applications. Constant air output, low noise, long service intervals. Ideal for manufacturing, automotive and food processing.

Reciprocating Compressors

Intermittent duty, lower initial cost. Piston technology for small workshops, construction and light industrial use. Not suitable for continuous 24×7 operation.

Centrifugal Compressors

Very high flow capacity. Centrifugal compressors are used in large-scale industrial air systems — power plants, chemical plants and steel mills. Highest efficiency at full load.

Portable Compressors

Mobile diesel- or electric-powered units. Construction sites, sandblasting, pipe laying and remote infrastructure work. Ranges from 50 CFM to 2,000 CFM.

Oil-Free Compressors

Essential for food, pharmaceutical and electronics manufacturing where oil contamination is unacceptable. Higher purchase price but lower maintenance downstream.

Medical / Breathing Air Compressors

Scuba diving, hospital air systems, beverage bottling and pharmaceutical cleanrooms. Demand the highest purity standards and certified component validation.

Buyer's Guide

How to source the right compressor without overpaying.

1

Calculate the required CFM and pressure rating.

CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) and pressure (PSIG) are the two fundamental specifications. Operating above or below the design point reduces compressor life and efficiency. Never estimate — measure or calculate using air consumption data from your pneumatic tools.

💡 Tip: Share your air tool list or consumption data — we will calculate the exact CFM requirement.
2

Match the compressor type to your duty cycle.

Rotary screw for continuous 24×7 duty. Reciprocating for intermittent or backup applications. Centrifugal for very high flow (>1,500 CFM). Choosing the wrong type is the most expensive mistake in air compressor procurement.

💡 Tip: Tell us your daily run hours and shift pattern — we will recommend the optimal compressor type.
3

Check for air quality and filtration requirements.

Oil-free compressors are significantly more expensive than oil-lubricated units but may be required by your production process. Air dryers and particle filters add cost but are essential for many applications. Determine your air quality class before comparing prices.

💡 Tip: Share your application — we will advise on the necessary air quality treatment components.
4

Factor in connected load and installation.

A 75 HP compressor with full load efficiency of 85% vs 75% can save $3,000–$5,000 per year in electricity. Installation costs including piping, electrical work and vibration isolation are often overlooked but can represent 15–25% of the total investment.

💡 Tip: We quote on a total installed basis — no hidden surprises after the machinery arrives.
5

Compare prices across authorised distributors.

The same compressor model with identical options can vary 25–35% across authorised distributors in the same region. Send us the model number, options list and required accessories — we will show you where the real market price sits, without obligation.

💡 Tip: Model number and options list is all we need. Verified price by end of business day.

Determine the kVA rating based on connected load.

The kVA rating is the single most important specification. Add the running loads of all equipment (motors, compressors, lighting, HVAC) plus a safe margin for starting loads. Undersizing leads to voltage drop and equipment damage. Oversizing burns unnecessary fuel.

Standby generators run fewer than 200 hours per year. Prime power generators run continuously. The same brand offers different engine lines for each duty class — and the wrong choice dramatically changes both purchase price and operating cost.
